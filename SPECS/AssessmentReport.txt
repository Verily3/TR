LeaderShift™ PDF Design Spec (Minimal Version)
Color Palette

Black (primary text)

White (background)

One brand accent color (deep navy or charcoal blue recommended)

Avoid:

Gradients

Shadows

Decorative elements

Multiple colors per band

The color bands in the radar can use muted tones — but everything else stays restrained.

Typography

Two fonts max:

Strong sans-serif headline (e.g., Montserrat / Helvetica / Inter)

Clean body font (same family, lighter weight)

Hierarchy:

LeaderShift™ – Bold
Leadership Capacity Stress Test – Medium
Created by The Oxley Group – Small caps or subtle

White space is authority.

Radar Design

Thin axis lines

Clean grid

Subtle band shading

Solid Self line

Dashed Boss line

No drop shadows

No 3D effects

Flat. Modern. Executive.

Constraint Section

Large bold heading:

CURRENT CEILING

One short paragraph below.

No boxes.
No heavy graphics.
Let the words carry weight.

CCI Gauge

Simple horizontal bar.

Label:
Coaching Capacity Index

Numeric score on left.
Band label on right.

Minimalist.

Overall Feel

It should look like:

A private board-level document

Something a CEO would bring to an offsite

Something that belongs in a board book

Not like an HR survey printout.

Final Recommendation Before You Build

When you mock this:

Do not try to make it impressive.

Make it calm.

If it looks calm and confident, the content feels serious.

Sample Assessment.


Final Scale (Frequency-Based)
1 = Never
2 = Rarely
3 = Sometimes
4 = Frequently
5 = Consistently
Now every statement reads as a behavioral pattern under load.
Below is the finalized Version 1.0 wording using frequency framing.
________________________________________
VISION
Direction Must Hold Under Pressure
1.	This leader maintains clear long-term direction even when under significant pressure.
2.	Strategic priorities remain aligned with a defined future state.
3.	The team confidently articulates where the organization is headed and why.
4.	Decisions reflect long-term intent over short-term relief.
5.	Direction remains steady when challenged or resisted.
6.	Daily execution measurably advances a defined future state.
7.	This leader challenges others to think strategically about long-term impact. (CCI)
8.	Strategic direction shifts when short-term pressure increases. [R]
________________________________________
CLARITY
Structure Must Reduce Friction
1.	Role accountabilities are explicit and outcome-based.
2.	The 3–5 core results of each role are clearly defined and documented.
3.	Performance metrics are visible and actively reviewed.
4.	Ownership of results is unmistakable.
5.	Performance discussions are grounded in measurable outcomes.
6.	This leader eliminates ambiguity that slows execution.
7.	This leader requires team members to define clear success criteria before action. (CCI)
8.	Role ambiguity contributes to execution delays. [R]
________________________________________
TEAMWORK
Tension Must Improve Performance
1.	Leadership team members demonstrate trust under pressure.
2.	Difficult issues are addressed directly and in the room.
3.	Productive conflict strengthens decisions.
4.	Enterprise results outweigh departmental preferences.
5.	Cross-functional coordination is disciplined and reliable.
6.	Feedback is exchanged without political consequence.
7.	This leader elevates the level of dialogue during disagreement. (CCI)
8.	Significant issues are discussed outside formal forums rather than openly. [R]
________________________________________
CANDOR
Standards Must Be Protected
1.	Underperformance is addressed promptly and clearly.
2.	Expectations and standards are explicit and reinforced.
3.	Feedback is precise and tied to measurable outcomes.
4.	Difficult truths are communicated regardless of hierarchy.
5.	Performance data is openly visible and discussed.
6.	Mediocrity is corrected before it becomes normalized.
7.	This leader requires others to confront the gap between intent and impact. (CCI)
8.	Poor performance is excused to avoid discomfort. [R]
________________________________________
ACCOUNTABILITY
Commitments Must Mean Something
1.	Commitments include clear deadlines and measurable outcomes.
2.	Coaching conversations include defined follow-up checkpoints.
3.	Progress toward goals is reviewed on a disciplined cadence.
4.	Missed commitments trigger corrective action.
5.	This leader takes full responsibility for results in their scope.
6.	Repeated underperformance leads to visible consequences.
7.	This leader requires others to state explicit commitments before closing discussions. (CCI)
8.	Missed commitments are tolerated without meaningful follow-up. [R]
________________________________________
CHANGE
Improvement Must Be Continuous
1.	The leader updates thinking when evidence warrants it.
2.	Underperforming systems are redesigned without delay.
3.	Change initiatives are structured and completed.
4.	Performance improves quarter over quarter.
5.	Experimentation is encouraged when results plateau.
6.	The status quo is challenged when it limits growth.
7.	During change, this leader pushes others to generate forward-thinking solutions. (CCI)
8.	Existing systems are defended despite declining results. [R]
